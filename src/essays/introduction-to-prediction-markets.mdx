Prediction markets are a new type of financial market that allow you to trade on the outcome of events. For example, you can bet on which team will win the FIFA World Cup of 2026 (tho my personal bet is on France again).

There are multiple prediction markets' platforms where you can bet on events. The most famous one is [Polymarket](https://polymarket.com/), but there are also other platforms like [Kalshi](https://kalshi.com/), [HyperLiquid](https://hyperfoundation.org/), and more. Each one has its own characteristics.

In this essay, I'll explain what prediction markets are, how they work, and what we're building with [Radion](https://radion.app/) to improve traders' experience.

## The taxonomy of prediction markets

As I said, a prediction market allows you to trade on events. But first, I may define what is an event.

An event is simply the combination of multiple outcomes. Most markets are binary markets meaning they can either resolve to **YES** or **NO**. But, some markets can have multiple outcomes. Think back about the FIFA World Cup example, the winner could be France, Spain, Portugal or any other country.
